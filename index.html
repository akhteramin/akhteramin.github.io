I work at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Linguistics to design, evaluate, and improve captioning technology and assistive technologies for people who are Deaf and Hard of Hearing(DHH).
I am currently a third-year Ph.D. student in Computing and Information Sciences at Rochester Institute of Technology and a graduate research assistant at the Center for Accessibility and Inclusion Research. My doctoral advisor is Matt Huenerfauth.
My primary research focuses on creating more usable captioning services for improving Deaf or Hard of Hearing(DHH) viewers' experience with TV programming. To achieve this DHH users' feedback was collected through different experimental approaches such as focus groups, semi-structured interviews, surveys. The analysis of this data has revealed DHH viewers' overall subjective preferences. Using the quantitative data, a caption evaluation metric has been built that can automatically estimate DHH viewers' subjective judgment of a captioned video quality. In another project, we are working on how to improve the user experience of American Sign Language dictionary for novice learners.
The ongoing investigations are as follows:

  1. Identifying caption occlusion severity among DHH viewers and quantifying their preference to build a metric using a supervised learning approach. Methods: semi-structured interviews
  2. Eliciting insights about DHH users' video caption style preference. Methods: Focus Group Sessions, Interviews
  3. Evaluating performance of existing caption evaluation metrics in terms of large caption transcripts. Methods: Structured Interviews
  4. Investigating DHH users' preferred speaker identification method while watching a captioned video in presence of multiple onscreen speakers. Methods: semi-structured interviews
  5. Introducing new modalities in existing sign language dictionaries to enable users to improve their ability to construct better query thereby improving their overall search experience. Methods: semi-structured interviews

In summer 2021, I have mentored 3 undergraduate researchers from Gallaudet University to conduct research e.g., study design, conducting interviews, data analysis, reporting the result, writing papers. Their summer internship has concluded successfully.
I am always looking to collaborate with new researchers and discuss shared academic or non-academic interests, so please drop me an email.
